About Holidae

We built this because holiday planning had stopped being enjoyable

Too many tabs. Too many price-comparison traps. Too little honest guidance on what a destination is actually like to visit. Holidae is an attempt to fix that — one curated destination at a time.

Who we are

A travel planning desk that respects your time and your intelligence

Where we started

Holidae started from a straightforward frustration: most holiday planning tools are built to generate bookings, not to help travellers make genuinely good decisions. The comparison tables obscure more than they reveal. The pricing hides until the last step.

We wanted something that led with destination character, was honest about pricing from the start, and moved you toward a decision without rushing you into one.

What we offer

Six destinations, each explained in terms of pace, mood, and who the trip actually suits. Three package tiers with transparent AED pricing throughout. A booking form that keeps your choices visible as you fill it in.

No hidden steps, no artificial urgency, no prices that only appear when you've already committed emotionally to the destination.

The planning journey

Three steps that feel like three steps, not thirty

Step 1

Understand the destination before you commit to it

Each destination page tells you who the trip suits, what the pace is like, what makes it genuinely distinct, and how it compares to the others in the collection. Browse with context, not just photographs.

Step 2

Compare packages without the small print obscuring everything

Essential, Signature, and Luxe are explained clearly — what's included, what changes between tiers, and which one makes sense for your trip and your priorities.

Step 3

Send an enquiry that actually has the right information in it

The booking form collects dates, room preference, board basis, traveller count, and any specific notes before anything is confirmed. You get direction rather than a vague reply.

How we approach it

The principles behind every page

These aren't aspirational values on a poster — they're the practical decisions that shaped how the site is built and how we talk about the trips.

Pricing clarity

The price is visible from the first page, not the last one

We put AED pricing on every destination and package page. You should know roughly what something costs before you've spent twenty minutes planning around it.

Honest guidance

We tell you when a destination isn't the right fit

Santorini is a slow island. Dubai is a busy city break. These aren't interchangeable. We'd rather help you pick the right one than push the most popular option.

Place personality

Every destination is described as itself, not as a template

Bali and the Maldives are both beach destinations. They feel completely different. Marrakech and Dubai are both UAE-region breaks. They also feel completely different. We try to honour that.

Our philosophy

The destination should feel aspirational. The decision should feel easy.

There's a version of holiday planning that makes you feel excited about where you're going, and another version that makes you feel like you're negotiating a complicated contract. Holidae is meant to be the first one.

That means giving imagery and explanation equal weight, being honest about what a trip costs and what it includes, and building a booking flow that reduces confusion rather than creating it.

Images that do more than decorate

Every photograph on the site is there to tell you something about the destination — the light, the setting, the pace. Not to fill a layout.

Copy that actually answers questions

Who is this trip for? What does the week actually feel like? How does it compare to the alternatives? We try to answer those before you have to ask.
